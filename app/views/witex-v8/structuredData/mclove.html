The circumstances are as follows;

On Saturday 12th February at approximately 1705 hours police were on plain clothes patrol in a plain vehicle on Tesco Car Park. At that time a black BMW 3 series estate, was sighted parked at the rear of DOMINO PIZZA in the car park. There was on a male on board the vehicle who was later identified as John JONES. LEGAL ASSESSMENT
PWITS

RPOC based on exhibits seized (drugs and paraphernalia), text messages and drug expert statement. Plus the fact that the officers witness a drugs transaction.

There is a moderate amount of cocaine seized - it arguably exceeds personal use. The packaging and configuration of drugs is indicative that the drugs were intended for onward supply.

S also has large amount of cash. There are messages on the phone seized from S which evidence that he is involved in supply. S admits that he has drugs down his trousers. He has cocaine in his hand and makes admissions on the PCE 10 that ASB9 is his – thus possession is not in dispute.

In IV, he did not admit supply but also failed to deny that he was involved in the supply of drugs.

I authorise charge for PWITS cocaine.

Possess Criminal Property

The quantity of cash is fairly consistent with the top value of the drugs seized, which indicates that S is dealing at the level suggested by the drugs expert.

This supports the prosecution case that the drugs are being supplied for financial gain.

Despite this, I am not considering a separate charge of possess criminal property – the presence of the cash suggests that S had a significant role, and should thus be treated as an aggravating feature of the drugs offences, rather than as a separate charge.

The cash has already been subjected to POCA. The amount being over £3000, this has been dealt with as a civil recovery, but the evidence suggests the money has been obtained through the sale of drugs

Other –

Whilst not a proposed charge, I did consider an offence of supply cocaine based on the transaction witnesses by PCs G and S. Ultimately, I do not consider there is RPOC. The officers didn’t see precisely what passed to the other male and he was never apprehended and so no items were recovered. Whilst it probably was drugs, and indeed S was found with cash and wraps of cocaine, it may muddy the waters to charge this offence.

In addition, it wouldn’t add anything to sentence. In the circumstances, I am content to treat this as a feature that strengthens the PWITS case.

There are no issues with the admissibility of the evidence. Nothing appears to undermine the prosecution evidence/assist the defence.

The evidence is all from police officers so is reliable & credible.

I am treating this as a GP –

A guilty plea may be anticipated where the suspect:
