Case summary

Provide a brief overview of the charges and facts in a case and the current bail or remand status of the suspect(s). You should also outline your rationale for applying the Threshold Test.

Notes for completing this section

You must be confident that all 5 conditions of the threshold test have been met. These are:

  1. there are reasonable ground to suspect that the person to be charged has committed the offence
  2. further evidence can be obtained to provide a realistic prospect of conviction
  3. the seriousness or the circumstances of the case justifies the making of an immediate charging decision
  4. there are continuing substantial grounds to object to bail in accordance with the Bail Act 1976 and in all the circumstances of the case it is proper to do so
  5. it is in the public interest to charge the suspect

Where any of the conditions are not met, the Threshold Test cannot be applied and the suspect cannot be charged.
