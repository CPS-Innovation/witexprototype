Claim witness expenses

Claim expenses for being a witness in English and Welsh criminal courts.

  2. Food and drink

    You can claim up to £9.75 per day for food and drink on the days you were in court. You don't need receipts.

    • Days away from home Not started Completed

  3. Travel

    You can claim for your expenses travelling to and from court. This includes train, tube, coach, car, parking, motorcyle, bicycle, air and ferry.
    You will need receipts to claim for train, coach, parking, taxi, air or ferry trips.

    • How did you get to court? Not started Completed
    • Cost of train tickets Completed
    • Cost of tube tickets Completed

  4. Overnight stay

    Everyone who stayed overnight can claim for a fixed amount to cover food and drink.

    If you paid for your overnight stay you can claim between £65 and £95 to cover the hotel cost depending on the location.

    Amounts you can claim

    Attendance overnight in London, Birmingham, Manchester, Leeds, Liverpool and Newcastle upon Tyne - £95.00

    Attendance elsewhere - £95.00

    The CPS will normally arrange the hotel accommodation for witnesses and pay for it. Where an evening meal is also included you will be entitled to the Personal Incidental Allowance only. Where no evening meal is included you will also be entitled to the Night Subsistence Allowance. These are fixed amount towards the cost of meals, refreshments, accommodation and other expenses where you had to stay away from home. The overnight allowance covers an absence of 24 hours and day subsistence is then only paid if you were away for more than 24 hours.

    Night Subsistence Allowance - £21.00

    Personal Incidental Allowance - £5.00

    If you stayed with family or friends, in place of the above, you can claim:

    Night Subsistence Allowance - £25

  7. Childcare

    If you paid someone to look after your children while you were at court and have an invoice from an Ofsted registered childcare provider you can claim for the full cost of childcare. If you do not have this you can claim up to £67 per day.

    What is Ofsted registered childcare?
    Ofsted inpect services providing education and skills for learners of all ages. Most childcare providers looking after children under the age of 8 must register with Ofsted (or a childminder agency).

    If you have a receipt from your Ofsted registered childcare provider it will have an Ofsted registeration number.

    You can check if your childcare provider is registered with Ofsted here: https://reports.ofsted.gov.uk/childcare
