Overnight stay

You can claim expenses for an overnight stay. Everyone who stayed overnight can claim for a fixed allowance to cover food and drink.

If you paid for your overnight stay you can claim between £65 and £95 to cover the hotel cost depending on the location.

Amounts you can claim

Attendance overnight in London, Birmingham, Manchester, Leeds, Liverpool and Newcastle upon Tyne - £95.00

Attendance elsewhere - £65.00

The CPS will normally arrange the hotel accommodation for witnesses and pay for it. Where an evening meal is also included you will be entitled to the Personal Incidental Allowance only. Where no evening meal is included you will also be entitled to the Night Subsistence Allowance. These are fixed amount towards the cost of meals, refreshments, accommodation and other expenses where you had to stay away from home. The overnight allowance covers an absence of 24 hours and day subsistence is then only paid if you were away for more than 24 hours.

Night Subsistence Allowance - £21.00

Personal Incidental Allowance - £5.00

If you stayed with family or friends, in place of the above, you can claim:

Night Subsistence Allowance - £25
